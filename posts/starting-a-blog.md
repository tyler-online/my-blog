---
title: "Why I'm finally blogging in 2026"
date: "May 13, 2026"
---

Why start a blog in 2026 (20 years after it was cool) that nobody is likely to read? It’s a good question.

The simplest answer is that I want to think more clearly.

The longer answer is below:

I want to think more clearly.
Paul Graham has long advocated for writing. He believes that writing is actually a critical part of the thinking process. Or at the very least, that writing increases quality, clarity, and originality of thought. I think I agree with him, but I’ve never written much (and certainly not publicly). This is an experiment to see whether I benefit from it.

I think it will be a good accountability mechanism.
I consume a lot of information: podcasts, tweets, essays, etc. But I don’t do enough with that information. Many perspectives I hold haven’t been fully explored or refined. Some perspectives I hold have turned out to be incredibly correct and early, but I didn’t act on them in any tangible way. I think writing may help correct that. I suspect that once I start publishing blogs, I will feel pressure to continue – and that pressure will be a forcing function to clarify thought and take action.

It may increase my surface area for luck.
It’s funny. On one hand, people talk about how much attention & luck comes from publishing a body of work. On the other hand, prolific authors and podcasters often say nobody cared about their work even after years of consistently publishing. I don’t expect many people to read my blog, but if it increases my surface area for connecting with likeminded people as a side effect – great!

Better late than never.
